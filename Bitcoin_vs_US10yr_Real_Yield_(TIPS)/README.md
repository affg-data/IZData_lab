# Bitcoin vs. U.S. 10-Year Real Yield (TIPS)

## Overview

Bitcoin is often viewed as a **risk asset** that competes with other investment opportunities for capital.

One of the most important macroeconomic indicators influencing this competition is the **U.S. 10-Year Real Yield (TIPS)**, which measures the inflation-adjusted return investors can earn on U.S. Treasury securities.

This project explores the historical relationship between Bitcoin prices and U.S. real yields to better understand how changing macroeconomic conditions affect cryptocurrency markets.

## Why Real Yields Matter

Real yields represent the **inflation-adjusted return** available on relatively risk-free U.S. government bonds.

Because Bitcoin does **not generate interest or cash flow**, investors often compare it with the real return available from Treasury securities.

- When **real yields rise**, investors can earn higher inflation-adjusted returns from government bonds, making non-yielding assets such as Bitcoin relatively less attractive.
- When **real yields fall** or become negative, financial conditions generally become more supportive of risk assets, including Bitcoin.

For this reason, Bitcoin has historically exhibited an **inverse relationship** with real yields.

---

## Key Observations

Several major macroeconomic periods stand out:

- **2020–2021 Quantitative Easing (QE)** – Real yields declined sharply while Bitcoin experienced a significant bull market.
- **2022 Federal Reserve Tightening Cycle** – Rising real yields coincided with a substantial decline in Bitcoin prices.
- **Changing Macro Regimes** – The relationship between Bitcoin and real yields is not constant, motivating the inclusion of a 365-day rolling correlation.

Across the full sample, the correlation between Bitcoin and U.S. 10-year real yields is approximately **–0.30**, indicating a moderate tendency for Bitcoin to move opposite to real yields.

---

## Key Takeaway

Real yields help explain an important part of Bitcoin's macroeconomic behavior.

When inflation-adjusted bond yields decline, liquidity conditions generally improve and investors often become more willing to hold higher-risk assets such as Bitcoin.

Conversely, rising real yields increase the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets and may place downward pressure on Bitcoin prices.

---

## Limitations

Although real yields are an important macroeconomic indicator, they explain only part of Bitcoin's price movements.

Other major factors include:

- Global liquidity conditions
- U.S. dollar strength
- Equity market risk appetite
- Cryptocurrency market cycles
- Institutional investment flows
- Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) inflows
- Regulatory developments

Accordingly, real yields should be viewed as **one component of a broader macroeconomic framework**, rather than a standalone predictor of Bitcoin's price.
